Crepes Suzette

Crepes Suzette are classic French crepes served with a delicious orange sauce. This elegant dessert is perfect for special occasions or a luxurious breakfast.

The crepes are light and thin, and the sauce is made with fresh orange juice, zest, butter, and a touch of liqueur for an extra kick. Serve them warm, and flambé the sauce tableside for a dramatic presentation.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons orange zest
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup Grand Marnier or other orange liqueur

Steps

  1. In a blender, combine flour, eggs, milk, water, salt, and melted butter. Blend until smooth. Let batter rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat a lightly oiled non-stick skillet over medium heat. Pour 1/4 cup of batter into the skillet, tilting to coat the surface evenly. Cook for about 1-2 minutes on each side, until golden. Repeat with remaining batter.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine orange juice, orange zest, sugar, and Grand Marnier. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring until sugar is dissolved. Reduce heat and simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. To serve, fold each crepe into quarters and place in the saucepan with the sauce. Heat briefly, then serve warm, optionally flambéing at the table for a dramatic presentation.
